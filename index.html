🚗 Reinstatement vs. Redemption Cheat Sheet

This cheat sheet helps Auto Collections Associates determine the correct resolution path based on loan status, sale status, and state-specific rules.

📘 Reinstatement (Pre-Charge-Off)

“Because your loan hasn’t charged off and we’re before the Legal-to-Sell date on your Notice of Intent to Sell, you can reinstate by paying your past-due amount plus applicable repossession-related fees. Reinstatement brings the account back into good standing and stops repossession activity.”

📕 Redemption (Post-Charge-Off)

“Because the loan has charged off, to get the vehicle back you must redeem by paying the full loan balance (including any allowed interest, fees, and repo-related costs) before the vehicle is sold. Redemption is available until the car is actually sold at auction.”

⚡ Fast Eligibility Check

  • 📉 Charge-off status?
    Not charged off → Reinstatement path may be available.
    Charged off → Redemption path.
  • 📆 Legal-to-Sell (LTS) date still open?
    Reinstatement allowed only until LTS (per NOI to Sell).
  • 🔍 Sale status?
    Redemption allowed until vehicle is sold.
  • 📜 State exception needed?
    Some states allow reinstatement even after charge-off. Check the state matrix first.

🛣️ Path Scenarios

✅ Reinstatement Path (Pre-Charge-Off; LTS not expired)

“Good news—because your loan hasn’t charged off and we’re still before the Legal-to-Sell date on your notice, you can reinstate by paying the full past due balance plus fees.”

🔁 Redemption Path (Post-Charge-Off; Until Sale)

“Because the account has charged off, the way to get the vehicle back is redemption—paying the full loan balance including any allowed interest, fees, and repossession costs. Redemption is available until the vehicle is sold at auction.”

⚖️ State-Required Reinstatement After Charge-Off (Approx. 11 States)

For accounts that have charged off and the borrower resides in a state that permits reinstatement (California, Illinois, Maryland, Mississippi, New York, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Washington DC, Wisconsin):

“In your state, the law allows a borrower to reinstate even after a charge-off under certain conditions. That means we can reverse the charge-off solely to process a reinstatement, as long as we’re before the Legal-to-Sell date and you pay the past-due amount plus applicable repossession fees.”

🧭 Quick Decision Guide (for the Agent)

  • ❓ Has the loan charged off?
    No → Reinstatement may be available until LTS.
    Yes → Redemption only, unless state requires post-CO reinstatement.
  • 🚗 Has the vehicle sold?
    No → Redemption still possible (post-CO) / Reinstatement still possible (pre-CO + before LTS).
    Yes → Neither reinstatement nor redemption is available; proceed with deficiency/closure workflow per policy.
